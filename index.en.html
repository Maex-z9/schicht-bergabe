The problem

What goes wrong at every shift change — costing time, material and nerves.

  • Notes disappear

    Slips end up under the vice, in the locker, or in the bin. The next shift starts blind.

  • Language barriers

    Polish, Turkish, Romanian, German — written in whatever works. Understood in whatever's left.

  • Tool chaos

    Who's got the torque wrench? Which machine is still running? Half the shift goes into looking.

  • Knowledge gets lost

    What we spotted yesterday, no one remembers today. The same issues come back — again and again.

The solution

A digital handover log that speaks every language — and forgets nothing.

  • Automatic translation

    Write in your language, colleagues read in theirs. Works for DE, EN, PL, TR, RO, RU and more.

  • Structured templates

    Machine, job, tools, open points. Defined fields instead of free-form prose.

  • Acknowledgement

    Whoever takes over the shift confirms each item. Nothing slips, responsibility is clear.

  • Full history

    Every handover stays searchable. Recurring problems become visible instead of happening again.

  • Tool overview

    Who has what, which machine is running, what's down — at a glance, instead of running through the hall.

  • On any device

    Phone, tablet, workshop PC. In the browser, no install, no IT department needed.

Who's behind it

Shift Handover is built by a former precision machinist — eight years at the machine, then a career in IT. The problem of lost slips and misunderstood handovers is one I lived through. That's exactly why this tool exists: built by someone who knows how things actually run on the shop floor.
